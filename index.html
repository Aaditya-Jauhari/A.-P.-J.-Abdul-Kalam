A.P.J Abdul Kalam

15th October 1931

Missile man of India

➼ Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble background and rose to prominence through his contributions to the field of aerospace

➼ Kalam played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was often referred to as the "People's President" due to his approachable and inspirational demeanor. Before his presidency, he held various positions in the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

➼ Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was a highly respected figure known for his vision, integrity, and commitment to education. Even after his term as President, he continued to inspire students and the youth through his speeches and writings, promoting education, innovation, and values. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire generations in India and beyond.

➼ Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born into a Tamil Muslim family, and his early life was marked by financial struggles. Despite these challenges, he excelled in academics and graduated in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology. Kalam played a crucial role in India's space exploration and military missile development, particularly in the development of ballistic missile technology.

He was intimately involved in India's first satellite launch vehicle and the development of the indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV). His leadership in the successful testing of the Agni and Prithvi missiles solidified his reputation as a key figure in India's defense and space capabilities.

➼ Apart from his contributions to science and technology, Dr. Kalam was a passionate advocate for education. He believed that education was the key to a nation's progress and development. Even during his presidency, he continued to connect with students and young people, emphasizing the importance of learning, innovation, and creativity.

➼ Dr. Kalam authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," where he shared his life experiences and vision for a developed India. His engaging and motivational speeches, often directed at students, earned him the nickname "People's President."
➼ After his term as President, Kalam remained an active public figure, participating in various educational and societal initiatives. His sudden demise while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong in 2015 was a profound loss for India. However, his teachings and legacy endure, continuing to inspire individuals to pursue knowledge, innovation, and a sense of responsibility towards society.

biographies

  • "Wings of Fire: An Autobiography" by A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: This is Dr. Kalam's autobiography, which provides insights into his early life, education, and career, highlighting his journey from a small town in Tamil Nadu to becoming the President of India.
  • "My Journey: Transforming Dreams into Actions" by A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: Another autobiography by Dr. Kalam, this book delves into his experiences, values, and the vision he had for the future of India.
  • "Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India" by A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: In this book, Dr. Kalam shares his thoughts on how India can achieve its full potential by unlocking the latent talents of its people.
  • "My Life: An Illustrated Autobiography" by A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: This book combines text with illustrations to narrate Dr. Kalam's life story, making it more accessible and engaging.
